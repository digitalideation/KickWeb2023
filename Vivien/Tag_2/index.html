MOST OF THE MATTER in the Universe consists
of stuff we can't see. It is dubbed “dark matter” and
we know it must be out there. Without dark matter
rapidly spinning galaxies (such as those circled, left)
would not have sufficient gravitational glue to hold
their stars and gas clouds together. These elements
would fly off into space instead, like rain drops on
a spinning bicycle wheel. What might this ghostly,
galaxy glue be made of? Nobody knows. But in 2006
astronomers got a new clue.
